## Phase 1 — Build (Weeks 1–10)

### Week 1
- [ ] Research and shortlist 3 co-packers (Makers Nutrition, NutraScience Labs, Supplement Manufacturing Partners)
- [ ] Create Amazon Seller Central account at sell.amazon.com ($39.99/mo)
- [ ] Purchase a single UPC barcode at gs1us.org (~$30)

### Week 2
- [ ] Email all 3 co-packers requesting a quote for 300–500 units of a 2-ingredient powder (Creatine Monohydrate 5g + Magnesium Glycinate 300mg per serving, 30 servings per pouch)
- [ ] Start brainstorming 5–10 brand name ideas
- [ ] Browse competitor listings on Amazon: search "creatine magnesium powder" and study top 5 listings

### Week 3
- [ ] Review co-packer quotes and select manufacturer
- [ ] Sign contract / pay deposit to co-packer
- [ ] Choose your brand name
- [ ] Post a label design brief on Fiverr or 99designs ($150–300 budget)

### Week 4
- [ ] Approve formula spec with co-packer
- [ ] Review initial label design concepts, provide feedback
- [ ] Research Amazon keywords using free tools (Amazon search bar suggestions, Helium10 free tier)

### Week 5
- [ ] Approve final label design
- [ ] Submit approved label to co-packer
- [ ] Draft Amazon listing: product title, 5 bullet points, product description
- [ ] Open a separate business bank account for the brand

### Week 6
- [ ] Register business name (LLC recommended, ~$50–150 depending on state)
- [ ] Get product liability insurance quote (required for Amazon, ~$500–800/year)
- [ ] Finalize Amazon listing draft — title, bullets, keywords, description

### Week 7
- [ ] Follow up with co-packer on production timeline
- [ ] Plan product photography — gather props, white background, good lighting (DIY or hire)
- [ ] Create brand social media handles (Instagram + TikTok) — even if not active yet

### Week 8
- [ ] Take or receive product photography (use prototype packaging if product not ready)
- [ ] Upload photos and complete Amazon listing
- [ ] Set up Amazon FBA shipment plan (ready to ship once product arrives)

### Week 9
- [ ] Follow up with co-packer — confirm shipping date
- [ ] Enroll in Amazon Brand Registry (requires trademark — start application now, ~$250–350)
- [ ] Research Amazon Vine program enrollment requirements

### Week 10
- [ ] Product arrives — inspect quality, count units, check labeling
- [ ] Request Certificate of Analysis (COA) from co-packer
- [ ] Create FBA shipment and ship inventory to Amazon warehouse

### Week 11
- [ ] Confirm inventory received at Amazon FBA warehouse
- [ ] Activate Amazon listing — go live

- [ ] Launch PPC campaign at $5/day — target keywords: "creatine magnesium powder", "magnesium glycinate creatine", "recovery supplement powder"

### Week 12
- [ ] Check PPC dashboard — review impressions, clicks, spend
- [ ] Respond to any early customer questions or messages
- [ ] Share product link with friends/family for honest feedback

### Week 13 — First Sales Target 🎯
- [ ] Review first sales and review activity
- [ ] Adjust PPC bids — pause keywords with no clicks, increase budget on converting ones
- [ ] Note any listing copy improvements based on customer language

### Week 14
- [ ] Review Vine reviews — note any product feedback
- [ ] Update listing bullets/description based on real customer language
- [ ] Identify top 5 converting keywords and increase bids

### Week 15
- [ ] Check inventory levels — reorder if selling faster than expected
- [ ] Compile list of 10 micro-influencers (fitness creators, 5k–50k followers on Instagram/TikTok)
- [ ] Draft outreach message offering free product for honest review

### Week 16
- [ ] Send product outreach to 10 micro-influencers
- [ ] Scale PPC budget to $10/day if ACOS is below 30%
- [ ] Review monthly P&L — units sold, revenue, net profit
